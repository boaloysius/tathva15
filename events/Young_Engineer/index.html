YOUNG ENGINEER

YOUNG ENGINEER

PRAAJNA

Engineering stimulates the mind. You have got to get out and air the imagination within, make things ,dismantle things ,fix things. Young engineer brings you the opportunity to rejuvenate by bringing forth a perfect blend of science, technology and entertainment.

RULES

The event comprises of two rounds :

PRELIMINARY ROUND
The test will be conducted at your school on October 3, 2015 in groups of two. This round will be a written test.It will contain questions that test the general engineering aptitude of participants.Qualified students can attend the final round conducted during Tathva .
FINAL ROUND
This round , test your Engineering quotient with practical hands - on tasks. It will consist of a series of practical problems for which the participants should find and implement a solution with the resources that are provided to them.
eg: Making a parachute for a boiled egg !
Come here as a team of two, get done the jobs given to you and the prize is yours...
No prerequisites required, no need to bring anything.All materials required will be given to you at the venue.

PROJECT EXPO

If you're itching to showcase your skills to the world and feel you possess the creativity to back it up, then this is the event for you. Present a project that portrays everything mentioned above. Use it to battle it out with your peers and command the centre stage this Tathva.

RULES

This competition is exclusively for school students from grade 8th to 12th.

A stage is set for you to transform your dreams into reality. All you have to do is bring your project and justify it with your presentation skills. You will be judged accordingly.

PRELIMINARY

  • Preliminary round will be an online abstract submission.
  • All students interested should send in your abstracts on or before  28th October.
  • Abstract should contain a description of the exhibit, your name and contact details.
  • Best 15 submissions will be selected for the finals.

BONUS

Top 5 groups from the prelims will get direct admission to Praajna, a hands-on practical challenge.

JUDGING CRITERIA

Projects adhering to current issues in the society and environment will assume more priority.

Try to incorporate an understanding of the five essential elements–knowledge, concepts, skills, attitude and action.

Making the project on your own will be highly appreciated. If getting external help contributes to ignorance of the design of the project, it will be considered a demerit.
