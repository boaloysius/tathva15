CAD ART

CAD-Art is a software based competition of Autodesk Auto CAD(ver. 18.0 to 20.1) .The event is basically on 2-Dimensional (2D) design.You are introduced to the construction and production industry in a positive and challenging manner.

DETAILS

  • Event contains a preliminary screening test.
  • Test will be conducted on basic Engineering Drawing and Auto CAD knowledge.
  • People who pass the screening test are allowed to next round.
  • Questions may be related to civil engineering or mechanical engineering drawings.
  • Laptops will be provided at the venue itself. Incase of any insufficiency of laptops,competitiors are separated into batches.
  • All the stationary required for rough work are also provided at venue.

RULES

  • No extensions and plug-ins to Autodesk Auto CAD basic software are allowed.
  • The final soft copy should be submitted in the native file format (.dwg).
  • No soft or hard copies of shortcuts and tutorials are encouraged.
  • Use of Android Auto CAD 360 software for better purposes is strictly prohibited.

DESCARTES' SQUARE

It took 60 years for William Lambton and George Everest to complete the great trigonometric survey of India covering heights of even the Himalayan giants Everest and Kanchenjunga! Do you have what it takes to be a great surveyor, then step right forward.Descartes Square tests your knowledge on basic surveying and geometry along with your practical skills.

DETAILS

  • The preliminary round tests your knowledge in the subject through a series of questions and problems on surveying and logical thinking. 
  • Five top scorers of the prelims proceed to the second round.
  • The second round puts your practical skills to the test. You will have to solve a practical problem in the field with instruments given.

JUDGING CRITERIA

  • The marking scheme for prelims questions will be given in the question paper. 
  • Five Top Scorers will proceed to the second round. 
  • Your score in the second round will depend on how fast you are in each stage of solving the problem and how accurate your final results are.

RULES

  • There should be exactly two members in a group. No more no less.
  • Considering the first round is a written test, you know what not to do in an exam. Violators will be disqualified.
  • Decision of the judges is final.
  • Use of instruments other than which is provided to you is prohibited.

LINKIDGE

The first bridges were made from nature – as simple as a log fallen across a stream. The first bridges made by humans were probably spans of wooden logs or planks and eventually stones, using a simple support and cross beam arrangement. But when it came for larger spans, trusses were introduced, which is now, the most widely used structural designs, many a time being utilized as structural solution of choice of bridges, roofs, cranes, aircrafts and even robots and spaceships!.

PROBLEM STATEMENT

Use ice cream sticks to create a truss bridge that satisfies the given requirements.

DESIGN CONSTRAINTS

Span of the truss bridge should be between 400 and 450mm. If the span of the structure is less than 400mm or more than 450mm, then the team will be disqualified. Maximum overlap between 2 sticks must be only 50%.

RULES AND REGULATIONS

  • Each team will be provided with 400 icecream sticks and a limited quantity of adhesive.  
  • Measuring instruments (rulers), cutting instruments (blades, scissors) will be provided on request.
  • Participants are not allowed to carry any material of their own
  • Time limit is 3 hours for making the structure.
  • The team can consist of a maximum of 5 and a minimum of 3 members.
  • If the number of registrations exceeds 8, then a primary round will be conducted and the top 8 teams will be selected for the final round.

JUDGING CRITERIA

Judging will be based on the maximum load taken by the structure and the efficiency ratio. Also a weightage will be given for the mark obtained in preliminary round. preliminary round(MCQ) related to structural design will be there.
