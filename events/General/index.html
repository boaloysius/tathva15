INQUISITO VIRTUOSO

Create your own path, don't follow the trail blindly or else you may fall into a trap. Play the blame game, be ambitious because your life is at stake. What if your friends turn out to be your foes? The fear of death follows from the fear of life. Revealing too much leaves you with nothing. Start where you are, use what you have to investigate, analyze and find the accused.

RULES

    Minimum of 2 and maximum of 4 participants in a team.

    There will be three rounds in all.

    Round I
    A written exam testing your aptitude, forensic and observation skills. 8 teams will qualify for Round II.
    Round II
    Teams will have to search for more clues, collect evidences outside the crime scene. Based on clues collected, the teams will go further in Round III. 5 teams will be shortlisted for the final round.
    Round III
    Teams will analyze the final crime scene, reason it and present it in front of judges. Points will be given based on the reasoning and evidence collected. The rules are subject to change based on the circumstances.
    In case of tie, a new task will be given to the participants according to the decision of the judges. The decision of the judges shall be final.

INTERFACE

INTERFACE is a national level project development initiative,  organized by IEEE NITC Student Branch and Tathva 2015,  which provides a platform for budding engineers and innovators  to get their projects funded. Entries are invited from the working  model category under any stream of engineering .The  shortlisting will be based on the technical and creative content  of the project.

RULES

  • A team representing an institute can consist a minimum of 1 member and a maximum of 5 members.
  • Multiple teams can represent an institute.
  • Projects coming under any wing of engineering are welcome.
  • A team can submit a maximum of five different projects/prototypes.
  • The projects may be put up for public display.

The participating teams must submit an abstract to  interface@tathva.org on or before 25th October 2015. 

ABSTRACT

  • A detailed description of the project must be submitted in less than 200 words.
  • Other Medias can be used to describe the project clearly.
  • The phone number and email address of all the team members  must be submitted.
  • The name of the college and other necessary details should be  included in the abstract.

JUDGING CRITERIA

  • A preliminary round will be conducted based on the abstract  submitted to ensure minimum quality of the project.
  • The main evaluation procedures will be conducted on  the date  of the event, 30 OCTOBER 2015 , done by the distinguished  faculty of NIT Calicut.
  • Innovation and the extend of practical application are the  primary basis of project evaluation with a small weightage for  the project presentation.
